{{2008-09 Big Ten men's basketball standings}}The '''2008–09 Minnesota Golden Gophers men's basketball team''' represented the [[University of Minnesota]] in the [[college basketball]] season of 2008–2009. The team's [[head coach]] is [[Tubby Smith]]. This is his second year as Minnesota's head coach. The Golden Gophers played their home games at [[Williams Arena]] in [[Minneapolis]], [[Minnesota]].  The [[Big Ten Network]] included the team in a two and a half hour special that featured [[Midnight Madness (basketball)|Midnight Madness]] events from several Big Ten campuses on October 17, 2008; the Minnesota event to kick off the season was called "Tubby's Tipoff".<ref name=NMMfUUbt>{{cite web|url=http://www.latimes.com/sports/college/basketball/la-sp-madness17-2008oct17,0,3319514.story|title=No 'Midnight Madness' for UCLA, USC basketball teams|accessdate=2008-12-01|date=2008-10-17|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|author=Pucin, Diane}}</ref> The Gophers started the season on a twelve-game winning streak for the first time since the 1948–49 season.<ref name=winstreak>{{cite web|url=http://www.gophersports.com/ViewArticle.dbml?DB_OEM_ID=8400&ATCLID=3637849|title=Gophers Finish Non-Conference Slate Undefeated|accessdate=2008-12-29|date=2008-12-28}}</ref>  The team became ranked on December 22, the first time the team had been ranked since the 2002–03 season.<ref>http://www.gophersports.com/ViewArticle.dbml?DB_OEM_ID=8400&ATCLID=3636645</ref>

Recruit Royce White signed with the Minnesota Golden Gophers, but did not play due to shoplifting and trespassing charges. He transferred to Iowa State in July 2010.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.cbssports.com/collegebasketball/story/13633550/minnesota-standout-royce-white-signs-with-iowa-st|title=Minnesota standout Royce White signs with Iowa St|work=[[Associated Press]]|publisher=[[CBS Sports]]|date=July 12, 2010|accessdate=August 5, 2010}}</ref>
